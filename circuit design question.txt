
1.	Design a circuit to safely operate an LED using a battery, ensuring it does not get damaged.
2.	Design a circuit where a switch can control the ON/OFF state of an LED.
3.	Design a circuit where two LEDs are connected such that both must glow using a single current path.
4.	Design a circuit where two LEDs are connected such that failure of one does not affect the other.
5.	Design a circuit where LED brightness is intentionally reduced without changing the battery.

6.	Design a circuit where LED glows only when both switches are ON. 
7.	Design a circuit where LED glows if any one of two switches is ON. 
8.	Design a circuit where LED is OFF when switch is ON and ON when switch is OFF. 
9.	Design a circuit where LED turns OFF only when both switches are ON, and remains ON otherwise. 
10.	Design a circuit where LED glows only when both switches are OFF. 
11.	Design a circuit where LED glows only when exactly one switch is ON (not both). 

12.	Design a circuit where LED turns ON after a noticeable delay when switch is turned ON.
13.	Design a circuit where LED remains ON for a short duration after switch is turned OFF.
14.	Design a circuit where LED gradually increases in brightness when power is applied.
15.	Design a circuit where LED gradually turns OFF instead of switching OFF instantly.
16.	Design a circuit where two LEDs glow with different timing using RC components.

17.	Design a circuit where switching action and capacitor together control LED behavior.
18.	Design a circuit where LED clearly demonstrates both charging and discharging of a capacitor.
19.	Design a compact circuit (minimum components) to create a delay in LED response.
20.	Design a circuit where one LED responds quickly and another responds slowly using RC combinations.
